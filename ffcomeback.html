In early 2022, while FFIC was still ongoing, Free Fire was suddenly banned in India. What many believed would be temporary turned into a long and painful pause.

Official tournaments stopped instantly. Brands exited. Organizations dissolved rosters. Many players quit competitive gaming altogether, while others continued grinding with no income and no certainty.

For nearly two years, the ecosystem survived only because of community-driven efforts. Figures like Rocky Mamu (ROCKY) and RDX, along with a handful of tournament organizers, kept the scene alive using their own resources.

From 14 February 2022 to mid-2025, Free Fire esports in India existed purely on belief, loyalty, and blind hope.
